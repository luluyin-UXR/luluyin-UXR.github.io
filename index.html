Banking Business Journey Builder
Strategic B2B research for Servicing and OKYC product teams · Global Banking and Fintech, London
Customer InterviewsCompetitor AnalysisConcept TestingB2B FintechEnterprise Tool Design
Senior UX Researcher · 2024 to 2025

The Business Problem

At a bank, building business journeys (the workflows that define how tasks like customer onboarding run end to end) is a core activity for Servicing and OKYC product teams. The current process is heavily engineering-dependent. Product Managers spend significant time translating product requirements into technical specifications, creating repeated back-and-forth cycles, higher implementation risk and consistently long launch timelines.

Research Objectives

Design assumption under investigation: Empower Product Managers to build and test journeys independently via a no-code UI tool.

  • What does the current journey configuration workflow actually look like?
  • Which teams are involved at each stage, and what do they own?
  • Where do Product Managers experience the most friction?
  • What would they most need from a configuration tool?

UX Research for a Tier 1 Commercial Banking Platform
Uncovering redesign opportunities for a platform serving corporates, governments and financial institutions · Capco, London
Usability EvaluationStakeholder InterviewsCustomer Interviews (25)Survey (48 responses)Commercial Banking
UX Researcher · Capco · 2022

The Business Context

A major Tier 1 UK bank had a commercial banking platform designed for finance teams managing money across large organisations, other banks, corporates, governments, schools and charities. The platform had not been redesigned in over twenty years. The bank needed to understand exactly what was breaking down in the experience before committing to a full redesign programme. The platform is role and task-centred, with the admin function sitting at the heart of every other interaction a customer's team has with the product.

Research Questions

  • What specific aspects of the platform are causing the most friction for users?
  • How does the current platform affect users' ability to complete core banking tasks?
  • Which areas, if improved, would deliver the greatest overall UX impact?

Platform Usability Assessment, Key Pain Areas

Severity scores (1 to 5) derived from usability evaluation and customer interviews. Admin function scored highest due to redundant roles, overlong privilege lists and heavy dependency on bank support teams.

Redesigned Admin Model, Five User Roles

Critical Finding, The Admin Function

The Admin section stood out as the most urgent pain point. Redundant user roles, overlong privilege lists and high dependency on bank support teams were causing real operational errors with genuine financial consequences for customers.

Two Distinct Finance Team Mindsets

Large finance teams have clearly defined structures and need role-based access tied to specific responsibilities. Small finance teams carry overlapping responsibilities and need broader, more flexible privileges. Both were being served by the same one-size-fits-all admin flow.

Research Outcome

The research led to a simplified model: a role-based journey for large teams and a user-based journey for smaller ones, with five predefined user roles replacing the existing privilege lists. This was expected to meaningfully reduce bank support call volume and lower user frustration.

Gumtree Super Seller Study
Deep-dive into the mindset, behaviour and experience of high-activity sellers · Gumtree, London
Qualitative InterviewsBehavioural ResearchJourney MappingOpportunity Identification
Lead UX Researcher · Gumtree · 2025 to Present

Why This Study Mattered

Gumtree had not refreshed its understanding of active sellers for some time and was moving through a significant transitional period. As the lead researcher, I directed a comprehensive exploration of "super sellers", the most active, high-volume users on the platform, to surface their mindsets, behaviours and pain points. The goal was to find opportunities to improve the platform and extend the habits of successful sellers to a wider base.

Research Objectives

  • Understand what motivates super sellers and what keeps them active
  • Analyse how they approach listing, communication and sales
  • Map the full post-to-sale journey and identify friction points
  • Explore why sellers move to or abandon other platforms
  • Generate concrete product opportunities grounded in real behaviour

Opportunities for Gumtree, by Priority

Key Best Practice Findings from Super Sellers

Creating effective ads

High-quality photos with clear backgrounds, key information in the title (price, condition, size), and approachable phrases like "open to reasonable offers" consistently boost click and reply rates. Posting on Fridays and weekends is perceived to generate more responses.

Communication that converts

Prompt replies, sharing extra photos proactively and maintaining a warm but efficient tone all drive successful outcomes. Sellers who set email auto-replies during unavailable periods do not realise those messages are silently dropped, a gap fixable with a simple platform communication.

Safe and smooth transactions

In-person, cash transactions are strongly preferred because they feel controlled and final. Sellers need the same level of protection as buyers in shipping scenarios. Buyer protection fees on top of shipping costs are actively deterring sellers from using the service.

The Keeper of Paintings, National Gallery AR Experience
Cross-cultural UX research programme for a first-of-its-kind immersive gallery experience · Brunel University London x The National Gallery
Desk ResearchPractitioner Interviews (14)Parent Surveys (50)Children Focus Groups (24)Co-design WorkshopsField Testing
UX Researcher · Brunel · 2019 to 2022

The Project

A research and development initiative co-led by StoryFutures at Brunel University and The National Gallery, the first time the Gallery had applied digital immersive technology to its on-site visitor experience. Seven institutions from the UK and China collaborated to create a child-friendly experience that worked meaningfully across both markets. My role was to serve as the bridge between the UK and China teams, facilitating mutual understanding of markets, audience behaviour, production workflows and creative goals across two very different institutional cultures.

Research Questions

  • Which immersive cultural experiences are most successful globally, and what makes them work?
  • How do UK and Chinese cultural production teams approach immersive content differently?
  • What are the primary interests and preferences of children and families in both countries?
  • What type of experience should the National Gallery create to genuinely increase visitor engagement?

Three Research Phases

Phase 1
Competitive Landscape
Desk research on global immersive cultural experiences and practitioner interviews with 14 production experts across the UK and China.
Phase 2
Audience Landscape
Parent surveys with 50 families exploring children's museum habits and AR familiarity, plus focus groups with 24 children on interests and painting preferences.
Phase 3
Design Areas
Co-design workshops with children to shape the concept, then a field study testing the AR experience through observation, post-session discussion and usability surveys.

Testing Results, Experience Dimensions

Key Audience Insights

  • Children in both the UK and China had limited AR experience but strong enthusiasm for it once introduced.
  • Mobile and tablet games were strongly preferred over PC-based games, informing the decision to build a mobile-first AR experience.
  • Art galleries were perceived as less engaging than science museums because of static, non-interactive displays, a direct creative brief.
  • Boys were drawn to level progression and rewards; girls preferred avatar customisation. The final concept accommodated both without compromising either.

Cross-Cultural Collaboration Insight

UK teams prioritised contracts and research before moving into production. Chinese teams focused more on pre-sales and operated at a faster pace. Bridging these two working cultures required active facilitation of shared creative and strategic goals at every stage and a genuine investment in mutual understanding rather than assuming alignment.

Final Concept
The Keeper of Paintings

An interactive, gamified AR mobile app combining progression levels and a reward system. A neutral guide character, the Keeper of Paintings, leads children through an immersive journey to explore and appreciate artworks at the National Gallery. The story begins with the Keeper losing a magical colour palette. Visitors follow clues hidden inside the paintings to recover it, earning rewards along the way and ultimately earning the title of the new Keeper of Paintings.
